Abu Dhabi – Getting Around

Public Transport
Abu Dhabi relies mainly on a modern bus network operated by Abu Dhabi Mobility (ITC). There is no metro system. Buses connect the city center, Yas Island, Saadiyat Island, and suburbs such as Mussafah and Al Ain.

Payment & Tickets
The Hafilat smart card is required for buses. Tourists should use the anonymous Hafilat card. Tap in and tap out is mandatory. Contactless bank cards are not yet fully supported.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing
Official silver taxis are safe, metered, and widely available.
Recommended apps: Careem, Uber, Abu Dhabi Taxi App, Yango.

Airport Transfers
Zayed International Airport (AUH).
Best option: Official airport taxi (fastest and most convenient).
Alternative: A1/A2 airport buses (budget, slower).

Micromobility
Careem Bike operates docked bikes in limited areas (Corniche, Yas Island). Mainly leisure use.

Driving & Parking
Car rental is recommended for flexibility.
Toll system: DARB (automatic, no booths).
Parking is managed by Mawaqif and is paid in most central zones.

Walkability
Low overall due to heat and distances. Walkable zones include the Corniche and Yas Bay.

Sources
Abu Dhabi Mobility – https://admobility.gov.ae
Careem – https://www.careem.com
Abu Dhabi Taxi – https://taxi.abudhabi

Amsterdam – Getting Around

Public Transport
Integrated system operated by GVB: trams, metro, buses, and free ferries across the IJ.

Payment & Tickets
OVpay allows direct contactless bank card or mobile wallet payment.
OV-chipkaart still exists but is optional for tourists.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing
Street hailing is limited. Use apps or taxi stands.
Recommended apps: Uber, Bolt.
Only taxis with blue license plates are legal.

Airport Transfers
Schiphol Airport (AMS).
Best option: NS train to Amsterdam Centraal.
Alternative: Bus 397 (Airport Express).

Micromobility
Cycling is dominant.
Bike rentals: MacBike, Donkey Republic.
Scooters available via Bolt in limited areas.

Driving & Parking
Strongly discouraged.
Parking is difficult and expensive. Use P+R facilities if driving.

Walkability
Very high. Canal Ring, Jordaan, and De Pijp are best explored on foot.

Sources
GVB – https://www.gvb.nl
OVpay – https://www.ovpay.nl
NS – https://www.ns.nl

Bangkok – Getting Around

Public Transport
BTS Skytrain and MRT Subway form the backbone. River boats are essential for historic areas.

Payment & Tickets
BTS and MRT use separate systems.
MRT accepts contactless bank cards.
BTS tickets are bought per ride or via Rabbit Card.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing
Metered taxis are common but must insist on meter.
Recommended apps: Grab, Bolt.

Airport Transfers
Suvarnabhumi (BKK): Airport Rail Link.
Don Mueang (DMK): Red Line train or bus.
Best option: Rail Link for speed, taxi for comfort.

Micromobility
Limited. Not a primary transport option.

Driving & Parking
Not recommended. Heavy congestion and limited parking.

Walkability
Low due to heat and uneven sidewalks. Walkable areas near BTS corridors and parks.

Sources
BTS – https://www.bts.co.th
MRT – https://www.bemplc.co.th
Grab – https://www.grab.com

Barcelona – Getting Around

Public Transport
Metro, buses, trams operated by TMB. Metro is most efficient.

Payment & Tickets
T-mobilitat system.
Hola Barcelona Travel Card for tourists.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing
Black-and-yellow taxis are official and reliable.
Apps: FreeNow, Cabify, Uber (limited).

Airport Transfers
Barcelona El Prat (BCN).
Best option: Aerobús to city center.

Micromobility
Bicing is residents-only.
Tourists use Donkey Republic.
Rental e-scooters are heavily restricted.

Driving & Parking
Not recommended inside city. Parking is difficult.

Walkability
High. Gothic Quarter and Eixample are very walkable.

Sources
TMB – https://www.tmb.cat
Hola Barcelona – https://www.holabarcelona.com

Dubai – Getting Around

Public Transport
Dubai Metro, Tram, buses operated by RTA.

Payment & Tickets
Nol Card required for all public transport.
Silver Nol Card is best for tourists.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing
RTA taxis are reliable.
Apps: Careem (Hala Taxi), Uber, Bolt.

Airport Transfers
Dubai International (DXB).
Best option: Taxi or Careem.
Metro works if hotel is on Red Line.

Micromobility
Careem Bike in Marina, Downtown.
E-scooters allowed only in designated zones.

Driving & Parking
Car rental optional.
Toll system: Salik (automatic).
Parking varies by area.

Walkability
Seasonal. Walkable zones include Marina Walk, JBR, Downtown.

Sources
RTA – https://www.rta.ae
Careem – https://www.careem.com
