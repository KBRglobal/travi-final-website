# TRAVI Content Strategy

_Created: 2026-01-28_
_Goal: Establish Google authority as the go-to travel information source_

## Content Pillars (5 Core Topics)

### Pillar 1: Destination Guides

**Why:** Core product - comprehensive city guides
**Search Intent:** "Dubai travel guide", "things to do in Paris"
**Content-Market Fit:** Direct alignment with product

### Pillar 2: Attraction Deep-Dives

**Why:** High-intent searches, affiliate revenue opportunity
**Search Intent:** "Burj Khalifa tickets", "is Louvre worth visiting"
**Content-Market Fit:** 3,000+ attractions to cover

### Pillar 3: Practical Travel Tips

**Why:** Builds trust, answers real traveler questions
**Search Intent:** "best time to visit Tokyo", "Dubai metro guide"
**Content-Market Fit:** Differentiates from review sites

### Pillar 4: Travel News & Events

**Why:** Fresh content, return visits, real-time relevance
**Search Intent:** "Dubai events 2026", "Paris exhibitions"
**Content-Market Fit:** Daily updates capability

### Pillar 5: Comparison & Decision Content

**Why:** Consideration-stage traffic, high engagement
**Search Intent:** "Dubai vs Abu Dhabi", "Paris neighborhoods comparison"
**Content-Market Fit:** Helps travelers decide

## SEO Guidelines

### On-Page Optimization

- Title: Include [City/Attraction] + [Year] + descriptive phrase
- H1: Match title, include primary keyword
- Meta description: 150-160 chars, include call-to-action
- URL: Keep short, include primary keyword
- Images: Descriptive alt text, WebP format, lazy loading

### Internal Linking Strategy

- Every attraction links to parent destination
- Every destination links to related attractions
- Comparison content links to both subjects
- Practical tips link to relevant destinations
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")

### Schema Markup (Already Implemented)

- TouristDestination for cities
- TouristAttraction for attractions
- FAQPage for FAQ sections
- HowTo for practical guides
- BreadcrumbList for navigation
- Place for geographic entities

## Competitive Gaps to Exploit

### vs TripAdvisor

- Cleaner, faster-loading pages
- Information-first (not review-focused)
- Better mobile experience
- Structured, scannable content

### vs Lonely Planet

- More up-to-date content
- Better SEO optimization
- Interactive elements
- Multilingual from day one

### vs Travel Blogs

- Consistent quality across all content
- Comprehensive coverage (not just popular spots)
- Structured data, not just prose
- Regular updates (not publish-and-forget)
